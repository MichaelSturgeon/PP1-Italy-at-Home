Pistachio Cannoli

Recipe Description

Cannoli are recognized worldwide and they are one of the most iconic pastries of Sicilian cuisine, as well as the entire Italian cuisine. Long ago, Cannoli were made only during Carnival time, but nowadays, their popularity has spread so much that they are regarded as the perfect representation of the Italian dessert.


  • Prep time - 30 minutes
  • Cook time - 10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 125 grams Flour, all-purpose
  • 2 tablespoons Sugar, granulated
  • ¼ teaspoon Salt
  • 2 tablespoons Butter, cut into small pieces
  • ¼ cup Marsala Wine
  • 1 Egg White, large, lightly beaten
  • Cooking Oil, as needed
  • 1½ cups Ricotta Cheese
  • 60 grams Powdered Sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon Vanilla Extract
  • 2 tablespoons Pistachios, finely chopped

Method

  1. Heat a pot of cooking oil over medium heat to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Combine the first three dry ingredients in a mixing bowl. Add butter and egg white.
  3. Next, gradually pour in wine while stirring, ensuring that a dough forms.
  4. Knead the cannoli shell dough for 6-8 minutes by hand until smooth.
  5. Flatten the cannoli dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and chill for at least a couple of hours.
  6. Once the dough has rested, divide it into 8 equal parts.
  7. Use a rolling pin to get the dough to an even 1/4″ thickness.
  8. Cut circles out of the rolled dough.
  9. Wrap each circle of dough around a cannoli mold and dampen the connecting edges with a little water so they remain sealed.
  10. Fry the cannoli in batches on each side for 1-2 minutes.
  11. Remove the fried cannoli from the oil and drain them on paper towels.
  12. Carefully remove the shells from the molds.
  13. Combine ricotta cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract in a food processor. Blend until smooth and let the filling chill for 15 minutes in a fridge.
  14. Add the chilled ricotta filling to a piping bag and fill each cannoli shell.
  15. Garnish the ends with chopped pistachios, then serve immediately.

Tips

  • You can use premade cannoli shells to cut down on the prep and eliminate all the cooking time for this recipe.
  • If possible, for best results chill the cannoli dough overnight.
